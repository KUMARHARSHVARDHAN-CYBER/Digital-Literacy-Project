What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of social engineering attack where cybercriminals use legitimate entities (like banks, companies, or government organizations) to deceive individuals into sharing personal information, such as passwords, credit card numbers, or login credentials.

How Phishing Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

Preparation and Setup: The attacker creates a fraudulent communication, typically an email,call or  fake website, that closely mimics a legitimate one.

Baiting: The message contains a hook, such as a warning about account issues, a prize notification, or a sense of urgency to provoke action.

The Trap: The user is encouraged to click a link, download an attachment, or log in to a fake website or make some kind of payment.

Data Capture: Once the user enters their credentials or downloads malicious files, the attacker collects sensitive information or installs malware (like keyloggers or ransomwareor any other viruses).

Exploitation: With the stolen information, the attacker may commit financial fraud, identity theft, or gain access to corporate systems to stole confidential data.
.
Who is Usually Targeted?

Individuals: Especially those with online banking accounts, shopping accounts, or social media profiles or High-Profile People.

Employees in Organizations: Particularly those with access to sensitive business data or IT systems.

High-Profile or Key Personnel: In spear-phishing attacks, attackers target executives, administrators, or decision-makers with customized messages.

Phishing attacks can target anyone, but those less aware of security practices or who are overly trusting of emails and links are especially vulnerable.

Consequences of Phishing

Financial Loss: Unauthorized transactions or drained bank accounts.

Identity Theft: Personal information used for fraudulent account creation or purchases.

Data Breaches: Organizational systems compromised, leading to sensitive data theft.

Malware Infection: Computers or networks become infected with ransomware, spyware, or trojans.

Reputation Damage: Both individuals and companies may suffer from public loss of trust if information is exposed.
Phishing can have serious short-term and long-term impacts, affecting finances, personal safety, and organizational integrity. Awareness, skepticism of suspicious communications, and using security tools such as multi-factor authentication help mitigate these risks
