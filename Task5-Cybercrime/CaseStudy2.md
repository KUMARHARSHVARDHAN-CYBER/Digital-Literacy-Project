Prevention Checklist
Never disclose sensitive information unsolicited: Legitimate organizations will not request passwords, PINs, or full banking details via email or text.

Use official channels: Confirm suspicious requests through official websites or phone numbers.

Trust instincts: If something feels wrong or too good to be true, pause and verify.

Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) or passwordless authentication: Adds an extra layer of security against stolen credentials. 

Keep software and devices updated: Applies to operating systems, browsers, and security software to patch vulnerabilities.

Deploy email security measures: Use SPF, DKIM, DMARC protocols, and spam filters to prevent spoofed emails.

Utilize antivirus, anti-malware, and firewalls: Protect against malware that may be delivered via phishing attempts. 

Use strong, unique passwords: Avoid reusing passwords across accounts.

Regularly update passwords: Especially after a suspected phishing attempt. Combine with MFA for enhanced security.

Avoid sharing access codes or authentication PINs: These are sensitive information and should only be used directly by the account owner.
